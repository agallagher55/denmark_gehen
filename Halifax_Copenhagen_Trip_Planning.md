## 💳 WestJet World Elite Companion Voucher

### How it works on this route
- Valid on Econo, EconoFlex, Premium, PremiumFlex (not Business/Member Exclusive)
- Companion's base fare is replaced with a **flat fee**:
  - Economy international: **$399 CAD**
  - Premium international: **$499 CAD**
- Companion still pays all taxes, ATC surcharges, and fees on top
- No blackout dates on the World Elite voucher
- Must be booked before voucher expiry; travel can occur after
- Return leg from Copenhagen must be booked by **calling WestJet contact centre** (website only accepts Canadian departure cities)

### ⚠️ Key Finding: Voucher Does NOT Help on Econo for This Route

WestJet confirmed this at checkout with a warning message. Here's why:

| Component | Amount (round trip, 2 adults) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Base fare × 2 | **$256.00** (~$64/person/leg) |
| Other ATC surcharges | **$1,460.00** |
| Taxes, fees & charges | **$364.80** |
| Bags (free with card) | ~~$160~~ → $0 |
| **Total** | **$2,080.80** |

The base fare is only ~$64/person per leg. The companion voucher would replace that with $399 — costing you **$335 MORE**, not less. WestJet automatically detects this and flags it.

**This applies to all Econo dates** — higher-priced dates (e.g. $1,060 or $2,078) are driven by inflated ATC surcharges, not higher base fares. The voucher still can't help.

### When Does the Voucher Help?

**Premium fare only.** At $1,448–2,684/person, the base fare component is large enough that replacing it with $499 saves real money.

| Scenario | Est. Total for 2 |
|----------|-----------------|
| 2× Econo, no voucher | ~$2,081 ✅ (your best bet for this trip) |
| 2× Premium, no voucher | ~$5,368+ |
| 1× Premium + companion voucher | ~$3,500–3,800 est. |

Premium with voucher saves ~$1,500–1,800 vs. two Premium tickets, but still costs ~$1,400–1,700 more than two Econo seats. On a 7-hour overnight flight, Econo is likely sufficient.

### 💡 Recommendation: Save Your Voucher
Use the companion voucher on a **domestic or sun destination route** where the base fare is a much larger proportion of the ticket price (e.g. a Caribbean or cross-Canada flight). Average savings in 2024 were $480–585 on domestic/sun routes.

## 🎯 Points Redemption Strategy

**Current balance:** 28,036.02 pts = **$280.36 CAD** in flight value

### How WestJet points redemption works
- Points can be applied at booking on westjet.com to reduce the cash price
- Redeemed at a fixed rate: 100 pts = $1 CAD (on flights and WestJet Vacations only)
- Points can cover part or all of a booking — no minimum redemption
- Points cover base fare + carrier surcharges, but **not** government taxes/fees
- No transfer partners — WestJet points can only be used on WestJet

### Should you use points on this trip?

**Yes — apply points to this booking.** Here's why:

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Redemption value | Fixed at 1¢/pt regardless of route — no "sweet spot" to wait for |
| Alternative uses | WestJet flights or WestJet Vacations only — no higher-value option exists |
| Point devaluation risk | WestJet could devalue points at any time — use them while the rate holds |
| Impact on this trip | **$2,081 → ~$1,801 CAD** for 2 adults (saves $280) |

**Note:** You'll still earn points on the portion paid with the WestJet card, just on the reduced cash amount.
